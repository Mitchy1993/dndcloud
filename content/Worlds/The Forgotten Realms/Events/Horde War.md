The Tuigan Invasion, also known as the Horde War, was a significant event in the history of Faerûn. It occurred in 1360 DR when the Tuigan horde, led by the charismatic warlord Yamun Khahan, swept out of the eastern steppes of Kara-Tur and invaded the western lands of Faerûn.

The invasion caught the western nations off guard, and the Tuigan horde quickly conquered vast territories, including the ancient city of Zhentil Keep and parts of the nation of Thesk. The Tuigan warriors were renowned for their horsemanship and brutal tactics, which allowed them to overwhelm many of their opponents.

However, several prominent leaders and nations in Faerûn rallied to oppose the invasion. Heroes such as Elminster and Khelben "Blackstaff" Arunsun played crucial roles in organizing resistance efforts. Nations like Cormyr, the Dalelands, and the Shining South united their forces to push back against the Tuigan invaders.

The decisive battle of the war took place at the Fields of the Dead, where a coalition of western forces faced off against the Tuigan horde in a bloody conflict. Despite their initial successes, the Tuigan forces were ultimately defeated, and Yamun Khahan himself was killed in battle.

The Tuigan Invasion left a lasting impact on the nations of Faerûn. It led to significant political changes and shifts in power dynamics across the region. Additionally, it served as a reminder of the constant threat posed by external forces to the stability of the continent.
